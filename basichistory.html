Our Big Question: Is the Silk Road was a story of peaceful trade, and a rich history of religious and harmonious cultural exchange?

Some people who don’t know about the Silk Road would get tricked by its name. First of all, it wasn’t a
road that made of silk. Secondly, it wasn’t only a road; some parts of the Silk Road were actually
ocean-based. Furthermore, silk was not the only item to be traded along the Silk Road. And lastly,
there was not just one road but many roads that comprised the Silk road. There were many of them.
The Silk Road is not just a road but a “road” of globalization. It connected Asia first, and then Asia
connected to Europe. It connected to parts of Africa at the end. The Silk Road started in Chang’an in
130 B.C. It worked by trading between countries.
